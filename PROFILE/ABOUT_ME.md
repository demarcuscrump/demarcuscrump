## The Backstory

I'm a late bloomer by every traditional metric. 36. First degree. No CS background. No internships at FAANG companies. No LinkedIn posts about "day 47 of my coding journey."

But I've always been a builder. I just didn't have the tools until now.

My first creative love was photography. I could take photos that people loved — good composition, good light, good feeling — but I could never explain *how*. I couldn't teach it. I couldn't articulate the rules I was following intuitively. People would ask "how did you get that shot?" and I'd just shrug.

That creative side never went away. When I'm not building AI products, I'm usually imagining original anime and western comic characters, worlds, and storylines for my own entertainment. I don't create only because there is a market; I create because my imagination needs somewhere to go.

That same pattern showed up everywhere. I could see the end result. I could feel what was right. But the gap between vision and execution was always blocked by some technical skill I didn't have and couldn't learn in the traditional way.

AI closed that gap.

Not magically, but in a life-changing way. For the first time in my life, the speed of my output matches the speed of my ideas. I can research a new industry on Monday and have a working prototype by Friday. I can see a business problem and build toward a solution the same week. That's not a flex — it's just what happens when you remove the bottleneck between imagination and execution.

## How I Work

1. **Research obsessively** — I'll spend days understanding an industry, its pain points, its existing solutions, and where the gaps are before I touch any tool.
2. **Blueprint everything** — PRDs, architecture docs, UX flows, data models. The plan is the product. The code is just the manifestation.
3. **Use AI as a multiplier** — I don't ask AI to "make me an app." I give it a detailed architecture, specific endpoints, exact data shapes, and precise UX requirements. The AI executes my design.
4. **Test and iterate relentlessly** — I run the code, find what breaks, fix it, run it again. I don't stop until it works the way I envisioned.
5. **Make it professional** — Every README has badges. Every project has documentation. Every interface follows real UX principles. I refuse to ship anything that looks like a homework assignment.
